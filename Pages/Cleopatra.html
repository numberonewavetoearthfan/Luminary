About Cleopatra VII

    The oldest child of King Ptolemy XII Auletes, Cleopatra was the last queen of the Macedonian dynasty that ruled Egypt between 323 and 30 bc. Alexander the Great’s general Ptolemy, who became King Ptolemy I Soter, founded the dynasty of Ptolemaic rulers. Upon her father’s death Cleopatra, then aged 18, inherited the throne alongside her younger brother Ptolemy XIII. At first Cleopatra served as the dominant ruler.Cleopatra was deeply loved by her people, often described as patriotic and intelligent, with ancient Egyptian texts referring to her as "Philopatris," meaning "she who loves her country." While not conventionally beautiful, she captivated others with her charm, wit, and political acumen. Cleopatra's brilliance lay in her ability to adapt and reinvent herself for political advantage. During her reign, Rome's growing dominance over Egypt became evident. Although Egypt remained wealthy, the Ptolemaic dynasty relied heavily on Rome's support. Following the death of Cleopatra's father, the Roman leader Pompey was tasked with overseeing her and her brother to ensure Egypt's tributes to Rome were maintained. Cleopatra fled Egypt in 50 BC after her brother Ptolemy XIII seized power. She gathered troops in Syria and returned to challenge him but initially failed. In 48 BC, she allied with Julius Caesar, becoming his lover and using his influence to reclaim the throne. Ptolemy XIII drowned in the Nile, and Cleopatra, now married to her younger brother Ptolemy XIV, gave birth to a son, Caesarion, implying Caesar was the father. Cleopatra visited Rome but returned to Egypt after Caesar’s assassination in 44 BC.

Background

    Cleopatra VII Thea Philopator, born around 69 B.C., was the last ruler of the Macedonian dynasty in Egypt. This dynasty began in 323 B.C., after Alexander the Great's death, when his general Ptolemy became King Ptolemy I Soter. Over three centuries, Ptolemaic Egypt became a significant world power.Cleopatra's father was King Ptolemy XII, while her mother's identity is less certain, with some believing she was Cleopatra V Tryphaena, her father's sister. There is also debate about Cleopatra's ethnicity, with suggestions that she might have had partial Black African ancestry.Little is known of Cleopatra’s childhood, except that she was being groomed as the future leader of the country. Unlike the rest of her family, Cleopatra took great interest in Egyptian history and religion. She was the only member of her family to learn the Egyptian language. Cleopatra became co-ruler of Egypt at the death of her father in 51 BC. Egyptian law forced her to share her new role as pharaoh with Ptolemy XIII, her younger brother. In fact, she would never be the sole ruler of Egypt in name, sharing the official title with her brothers and her son.

Achievements

Restoring Egypt's Independence

Cleopatra navigated Egypt through political turmoil, maintaining its independence despite Roman pressures. She formed alliances with powerful Roman leaders like Julius Caesar and Mark Antony to strengthen her position.

Economic and Cultural Prosperity

Under Cleopatra's reign, Egypt experienced economic stability. She focused on revitalizing trade and bolstered Alexandria's status as a cultural and intellectual hub in the Mediterranean.

Strategic Alliances with Rome

Cleopatra’s relationship with Julius Caesar helped secure her throne and ensured her son, Caesarion, was acknowledged as his heir. Later, her alliance with Mark Antony provided military support against rivals.

Commanding Leadership

Cleopatra demonstrated remarkable leadership during wars, such as supporting Antony in the Battle of Actium, even though the campaign ultimately failed.

Facts

  • Unlike her predecessors, Cleopatra embraced Egyptian traditions and presented herself as a living embodiment of the goddess Isis, earning the loyalty of her people.

  • Cleopatra VII's full name is actually Cleopatra VII Thea Philopator
  • After her death on August 12, 30 B.C., Cleopatra was buried alongside Antony in a yet to be discovered location.
  • Cleopatra was determined to secure her throne, even resorting to having some of her siblings killed. She fought a civil war against her brother, Ptolemy XIII, for control of the crown. Aligning with Julius Caesar, she triumphed in the Battle of the Nile, leading to Ptolemy XIII's death in 47 BCE.
